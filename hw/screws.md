
### Cross-Recessed Head Screws

- Phillips
  - Approx 30° angle from vertical at tip.
- [JIS B 1012]
  - Approx 45° angle from vertical at tip.
  - screws identified by a dot or "X" next to cross slot
  - can be used on Phillips screws
  - obsoleted by nearly identical ISO 8767-1
- [ISO 8764-1] drivers for cross-recessed head screws ([ISO 4757])
  - nearly identical to JIS B 1012
  - PH for H recesses (look like Phillips)
  - PZ for Z recesses (look like Posidrive)

### Bit Holders, Bits and Shanks

ISO 1173 specifies bit shank sizes and designs. The major ones are:

Sizes ({minimum length]minimum length in {braces:
- __4 mm__ ("mini hex", "micro hex"). Min. len 9 mm. Used in common
  jewelers'/electronics multi-bit and driver sets. This may have a small
  spring-loaded ball in the shank to help a little in locking it, but most
  bits do not have this.
- __6.3 mm__ (1/4"): Min len. C=11mm, E=25mm. Used by impact drivers, power
  drills and 'regular' size nut driver hand tools and their bit sets.
- Other sizes include  7.94 mm (5/16"), 8mm, 11.02 mm (7/16"), 11.2 mm.

Shanks:
- __Form C:__ Bits often very small, 1-2 cm long. Friction or magnetic fit.
- __Form E:__ Larger bits with a detent for a ball in the driver, used as a
  latching mechanism to keep it from falling out. There are other similar
  designs, but since 2010 form E is the most commonly used.
